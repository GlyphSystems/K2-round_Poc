Phase 9 Summary — Logic Engine Economic Delta Analysis and Minimal Reproduction Candidate Mapping

Objective

Phase 9 was designed to supplement the protocol-level findings from Phases 4 through 8 with high-volume economic state analysis.

Earlier phases used the real K2/C4 protocol harness to validate repayment-boundary behavior, persistent large-repayment failure, collateral withdrawal under active debt, amount-sensitive repayment behavior, fragmented repayment recovery, and recovery-path limitations.

Phase 9 shifted into Logic Engine analysis.

The objective was to determine whether the observed repayment-boundary behavior had a broader economic structure behind it. Specifically, Phase 9 tested whether supply, borrow, repay, and withdraw sequences could produce deterministic accounting asymmetry, persistent residual debt, phantom debt-share formation, modeled protocol shortfall, or extraction-path candidate behavior under repeated and constrained economic state transitions.

The purpose of Phase 9 was not to replace the real protocol harness evidence. The purpose was to identify and compress candidate economic patterns that may explain, amplify, or reproduce the repayment asymmetry observed in the protocol-level phases.

Scope

Phase 9 focused on:

- supply → borrow → repay → withdraw sequence modeling
- debt-share mint and burn asymmetry
- repayment-size imbalance
- phantom debt-share persistence
- residual debt-value persistence
- modeled economic delta
- modeled protocol shortfall
- re-entry persistence
- replay-stable imbalance behavior
- boundary collapse conditions
- minimal reproduction candidates
- healthy-control counterfactual requirements

All Phase 9 findings should be interpreted as simulation-layer economic evidence unless and until converted into protocol-real reproduction inside the official K2/C4 harness.

Methodology

The Logic Engine was configured to evaluate large numbers of accounting-state transitions while preserving compact telemetry rather than raw per-cycle logs.

The engine generated:

- aggregate anomaly counts
- top-ranked anomaly hits
- modeled delta values
- phantom debt-share measurements
- phantom debt-value measurements
- signature compression outputs
- replay-stability measurements
- constrained-path candidate results
- minimal reproduction candidates

The engine intentionally avoided exposing proprietary implementation details. Output was limited to reviewer-relevant economic telemetry.

The core testing logic evaluated whether specific transaction structures could repeatedly create a mismatch between:

- supplied capital
- borrowed capital
- repaid capital
- withdrawn value
- debt shares minted
- debt shares burned
- residual debt shares
- residual debt value
- final modeled output
- modeled protocol shortfall
