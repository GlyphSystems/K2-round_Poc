Phase 5 Summary — Pre-Failure Coherence and Cycle 10 Boundary Reproduction

Objective

Phase 5 was designed to validate the repayment failure boundary identified during Phase 4 and determine whether the observed Contract #35 / allowance-related failure was caused by gradual account corruption, pre-failure state drift, or a specific reproducible execution boundary.

Phase 4 established that repeated patterned residual borrow/repay cycling could reach a repayment-path failure around Cycle 10. Phase 5 narrowed that finding by asking three core questions:

1. Does accounting remain coherent before the failure occurs?
2. Does stopping before the boundary leave the account usable?
3. Does re-entering the same boundary reproduce the failure deterministically?

This phase was not intended to prove final exploit impact. Its purpose was to separate pre-boundary accounting coherence from boundary-triggered repayment failure.

Phase 5A — Post-Revert Accounting Consistency Check

Observed execution:

The harness executed repeated patterned borrow/repay cycles. During successful completed cycles, the following pattern remained consistent:

This showed that the account did not show obvious corruption during the successful pre-failure cycles.

Phase 5A did not prove post-revert corruption. Instead, it strengthened the reproducibility of the repayment failure boundary while showing pre-failure accounting coherence.

Consolidated Phase 5 Findings

Phase 5 established three important conclusions.

First, accounting remains coherent before the failure boundary. Phase 5A showed that completed cycles prior to the failure return debt to zero, preserve collateral, and restore health factor to max / infinite after successful repayment.

Second, the account remains usable when stopped before the boundary. Phase 5B showed that after 9 patterned cycles, normal supply, borrow, and repay operations still work. This means the account is not obviously poisoned before the boundary.

Third, re-entering the Cycle 10 boundary reproduces the repayment failure. Phase 5C confirmed that the same sequence again reaches Contract #35 / allowance-related failure during repayment execution.

The combined result is that the failure appears boundary-specific rather than continuously corruptive.

Red-Team Assessment

Phase 5 should not be framed as final exploit proof.

Phase 5 did not prove:

direct value extraction
collateral inflation
debt disappearance
reserve theft
protocol insolvency
post-revert state corruption
cross-user impact
health-factor bypass

However, Phase 5 did prove:

the repayment failure boundary is reproducible
pre-boundary completed cycles remain accounting-coherent
stopping before the boundary allows normal protocol operations
re-entering the boundary reproduces failure
the user can have visible asset_b balance before the failing repayment
the failure appears tied to the Cycle 10 repayment path rather than general account poisoning

This distinction matters because it keeps the report credible. The correct claim is not that Phase 5 proves catastrophic loss. The correct claim is that Phase 5 isolates and confirms a deterministic repayment execution boundary.

Security Interpretation

The strongest technical claim from Phase 5 is:

Repeated patterned residual cycling reliably reaches a repayment failure boundary around Cycle 10, while completed pre-boundary cycles remain accounting-coherent and normal operations remain available when execution stops before the boundary.

This is a meaningful protocol signal because repayment is a core lifecycle operation. A reproducible repayment failure after a specific patterned sequence may create downstream risk if later phases show trapped debt, inability to repay, liquidation exposure, collateral withdrawal under unresolved debt, or reserve/accounting mismatch.

At the Phase 5 stage, those downstream impacts were not yet confirmed. Phase 5 served as the necessary boundary isolation layer before impact testing.

Relationship to Later Phases

Conclusion

Phase 5 successfully converted the Phase 4 repayment failure signal into a controlled boundary-confirmation result.

The tests showed that completed cycles before the failure remain coherent, and that stopping before the known boundary leaves the account usable for normal supply, borrow, and repay operations. However, re-entering the patterned sequence through Cycle 10 reproduced the repayment-path failure with Contract #35 / allowance-related diagnostics.

The strongest Phase 5 conclusion is:

The observed repayment failure is not caused by continuous pre-boundary account corruption. It appears to be a deterministic Cycle 10 repayment boundary triggered by repeated patterned residual borrow/repay cycling.

Phase 5 does not establish P1 impact by itself. It provides the credibility foundation required for later impact testing by proving that the failure is reproducible, boundary-specific, and not explained by general account poisoning before the boundary.
